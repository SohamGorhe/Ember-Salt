The Beginning

Born from a
single hearth.

Ember & Salt began in 2019 with a disagreement. Chef Marco Solano — having spent a decade cooking in the finest kitchens of San Sebastián, Barcelona, and later New York — left his position at a three-star restaurant on a single conviction: that the most honest cooking required the simplest tools.

He found a space on the edge of the coast. He installed one hearth. He hired a fisherman. He opened with thirty-two seats and a menu written in chalk.

The chalk board is gone now. The fisherman is still the same. The hearth has not moved. The philosophy has not changed.

What began as a 32-seat experiment in honest cooking has become one of the most talked-about tables on the Mediterranean coast — not because it changed, but because it refused to.

The Kitchen
Marco Solano
Head Chef & Co-Founder

Born in Valencia, raised on paella cooked over open wood fires by his grandmother, Marco spent his formative years in the kitchens of northern Spain — where Basque cooking taught him that restraint is not a limitation, it is a form of respect for the ingredient.

After stints at Elkano in Getaria and later at Eleven Madison Park in New York, he returned to the Mediterranean coast with a clear purpose: to cook the food of the sea, over fire, without apology.

What We Believe

Four things we refuse to compromise on.

I
The Ingredient

Nothing arrives at our table that did not begin its day somewhere extraordinary. We pay above market rate for fish caught the same morning. We refuse preserved, farmed, or out-of-season produce in our main courses. The ingredient is the dish.

II
The Fire

We cook over wood. Specifically olive wood, sourced from orchards in the south. The smoke it gives is different from oak, from cherry, from applewood. It is coastal, warm, and faintly sweet — and it belongs with the sea.

III
The Pace

We do not turn tables. We do not set time limits. A table at Ember & Salt is yours for the evening. If you are still here when the kitchen closes, we will find something to open. A meal is not an appointment.

IV
The Honesty

If the fish did not arrive today, we will not serve it. If the burrata is not right, we will tell you before you order. We have sent dishes back to the kitchen in front of guests. We will do it again. The standard does not move.
