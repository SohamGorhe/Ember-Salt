Our Philosophy

The table is not a destination.
It is a conversation —
between sea and soil,
fire and salt.

At Ember & Salt, we believe that the finest ingredients need only the simplest intention — good fire, good salt, and time. Our kitchen is built around a wood-fired hearth imported from the Basque Country, where every dish passes through flame before it meets the plate.

The sea is our larder. Each morning our team selects from what the water offers — sea bass pulled from the coast at dawn, squid that turns to silk over olive wood, langoustines still alive in the crate.

What we offer is not a performance. It is a meal, in the truest sense — shared, warm, and unhurried.

"The sea does not hurry. Neither do we."

Ember & Salt was born from a simple disagreement with the modern restaurant — that dining should be efficient, curated, optimised. We believe the opposite. A meal should be slow enough to notice the light changing.

Our wood-fired hearth is not a cooking technique. It is a declaration: that some things are better when they take longer, when they carry the smell of smoke and the warmth of a real fire.

Every morning our chef walks the market. The menu follows whatever arrived overnight — a crate of live langoustines, an unexpected delivery of early-season figs, a fisherman's surplus of sea bass. We write the menu around what is real, not what is planned.

Sit. Order too much. Stay too long. That is the only reservation we ask you to honour.

— Chef Marco Solano, Head of Kitchen
